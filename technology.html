StethAid

Automated Point of Care Analysis of Still's Murmur.

Clinical Problem

  • Still’s murmur is the most common innocent (benign) heart murmur of childhood, occurring in 50%–90% of healthy children.
  • It's incidence exceeds the incidence of heart murmurs of actual congenital heart defects in the pediatric population.
  • Still’s murmur does not require any treatment; it resolves on its own by adolescence.
  • Primary care physicians (PCPs), pediatricians included, often lack the skills to distinguish Still’s murmur from pathological murmurs.
